This is Biryani

Description

Biryani is a popular and flavorful dish that originated in South Asia, particularly in the Indian subcontinent. It is a mixed rice dish made with aromatic basmati rice, various spices, and meat (such as chicken, mutton, beef, or fish), or vegetables. The dish is known for its rich and fragrant blend of spices, which may include cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, and bay leaves, among others.

There are several regional variations of biryani, each with its unique preparation style and combination of ingredients. Some common types of biryani include Hyderabadi biryani, Lucknawi biryani, Kolkata biryani, and Malabar biryani.

The rice and meat or vegetables are typically cooked together, allowing the flavors to blend during the cooking process. Biryani is often garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and occasionally boiled eggs. It is a popular and beloved dish in many parts of South Asia and has gained international recognition for its delicious and aromatic taste
